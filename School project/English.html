ILA SACHANI

INTEGRATED ENGLISH PROJECT

Once there was a cry heard in a small room in Moti Vavdi village, Bhavnagar, Gujarat .A little girl named Ila sachani was born. She was born into a farming family. Despite being born with her hands she could not use conventionally, she was determined to overcome her challenges. Her mother and grandmother, skilled in Kathiawar embroidery, taught her to create stunning patterns using her feet.
Initially, threading a needle was a very difficult task for Ila, but with unwavering determination, she became an expert at a young age. She also learned various styles like Kachhi, Kashmiri, and Lucknawi.

As word spread, her work gained recognition in the village, leading to a display of her embroidery at a state exhibition in Surat.Encouraged by this success, Ila participated in more exhibitions, eventually earning national recognition and prestigious awards, including the President’s Medal.
Ila’s embroidery, crafted with her feet, serves as a powerful reminder that art transcends physical limitations and has the ability to touch the soul, inspiring all who witness her remarkable journey
